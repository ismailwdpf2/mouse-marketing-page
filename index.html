Introduction: Magnito Mouse adopts a novel concept: taking advantage of magnetism, it allows a flat-top mouse to adhere to one side of a separate, thin pad the other side of which is glued to stick on anything you may affix it to. First Impression: Magnito Mouse is a medium size mouse powered with two AAA batteries. It sports a pleasing build and heft (batteries inserted) and an unusual shape because of the flat top. The mouse (including batteries) weights approximately 3.2 ounces. The adhesive and magnetic pad is just shy of an additional 0.5 ounces. When affixed to a device, you'll be lugging around 3.5 to 4 ounces extra weight. The attached photos and movie show some features of this mouse. Operation: Magnito Mouse can connect to two Bluetooth hosts and switch between them for pairing. Connection and pairing with two machines (Windows 10 or 11) was effortless. Magnito was recognized in all cases as a BT 5 device. The mouse has a sleep mode that kicks in automatically. Shifting the mouse will not (hurrah!) wake the mouse up. For that, a click is necessary. The mouse slides adequately on a mouse pad; resistance to sliding is OK, but higher than for a twice-the-price Logitech MX 2s Anywhere mouse. Sensitivity, for me, is fine out of the box. Magnito is almost a perfect ambidextrous mouse. The flat top is unusual but not crippling for my semi-claw grip. If you use a palm grip, you may find the shape of this mouse hard to get used to. Clicking is precise and the tactile feedback is pleasant and clearly perceptible. The mouse wheel is also a middle button, and will tilt left and right, but does not offer hyper-scrolling. The tactile feedback of the wheel's clicking detents is competent. Overall: on Windows, a well-priced, competent no-frills mouse with good connectivity and pleasant enough ergonomics (for a semi-claw gripper, read above for palm grips). If you appreciate Magnito's new concept for portability, it's a winner. As a non-portable desktop mouse it's OK, but this circumstance is not its forte.

Frequently Asked Questions

How strong is the magnet?
Magnito’s magnet is stronger than the Apple Pencil, and if you use the Magnito Pad, you can get an even stronger grip from the magnet. If you’re using Magnito with a much older model device, or a low-price tablet such as the Galaxy Tab A, the magnet may have a weaker hold. We recommend using the Magnito Pad for these products.
Does it attach well to tablet cases as well?
Tablet cases will cover the magnetic power, which will make it harder to attach. If you buy Magnito Pad, Add-ons item, and attach it to a tablet case, the case will hold Magnito with much stronger adhesion. Attaching Magnito to even laptops (with less magnetism) is possible.
How to use remote-controller mode?
Run camera/Media apps on tablets or smartphones connected to Magnito, and you can remotely control them. For PPT, move to “Slide Show” mode and control them.
How can I get the Magnito Pad?
The Magnito Pad is included by default in the Magnito product package. If you need additional Magnito Pads, you can select them in the Add-ons section for an additional fee.
